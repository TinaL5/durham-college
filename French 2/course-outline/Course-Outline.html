Course Description

This course integrates and extends previously introduced concepts of French 1. The course aims to develop competencies in French through authentic learning tasks in preparation for real-life application. Online activities will promote skill development in listening, speaking, reading, and writing in personal and professional contexts. You will use communication strategies to support interpersonal skills, to convey information, and to interact with various forms of persuasive exchanges in routine situations. You will develop skills for life-long learning that are transferable to employment. The targeted second language proficiency of the course aligns with level 3 requirements of the Canadian Language Benchmarks for French, the NCLC.

Eligibility for Prior Learning Assessment & Recognition (PLAR)

Prior Learning Assessment and Recognition (PLAR) is a process a student can use to gain college credit(s) for learning and skills acquired through previous life and work experiences. Candidates who successfully demonstrate mastery of the course competency(ies) may be granted a “pass” for the course. The type of assessment method (s) used will be determined by subject matter experts. Because this is a pass/fail course, the PLAR will be assessed on this basis.

Competencies

A competency is an observable behaviour that successful performers demonstrate on the job. This behaviour is the result of a combination of related knowledge, skills and attributes. Listed below are both the module development indicators (for context) and the course competency.

Course Competency Statement:

Communicate in routine, predictable situations to deal with known topics of immediate personal interest.

Development Indicators:


Module 1: Interpersonal Relationships and Communication Strategies

Apply basic listening, speaking, reading and writing communication strategies in formal and everyday situations to foster interpersonal relationships.

Module 2: Information and Communication Strategies

Apply basic listening, speaking, reading and writing communication strategies in formal and everyday situations to interpret and respond to information of immediate relevance in various contexts.

Module 3: Persuasive Communication Strategies

Apply basic listening, speaking, reading and writing communication strategies in formal and everyday situations to persuade others to act and consider ideas in the context of familiar situations.

Evaluation Criteria

Formative feedback will be provided through online activities and by the facilitator on selected activities. Formative feedback will not be graded and is meant to guide the learning process. In addition to formative feedback, the facilitator will observe demonstration of module development indicators on an integrated group of tasks more formally towards the end of the module through a formative assessment. A goal of the formative assessment is to prepare students for the summative assessment, and observations based on evaluation criteria will be recorded on a rubric that will also be used for the summative assessment; there is only one submission for the formative assessment, which is mandatory. Neither the formative feedback nor the formative assessment will factor into the final course pass/fail outcome but will instead help the student monitor their learning progress through the course.

As a competency-based course, the course’s overall pass/fail assessment will be based solely on the three module summative assessments of integrated groups of authentic tasks, which the student will be required to complete to demonstrate their mastery of the course competency. A passing grade is set at 70%. If a pass is not earned the first time the student attempts the a module assessment, a second attempt will be permitted. If the second attempt is not successful, the student will be required to re-take the course in its entirety at the student’s cost.

Important Note:

The final summative assessment must be successfully completed by the course end date. No extensions to this date will be provided unless accompanied in advance by an approved accommodation form. Therefore, students are strongly encouraged to attempt this final assessment at least two weeks prior to the course end date, in order to ensure sufficient time is available if a second attempt is required.

Your final course grade will be reported as a letter grade. After all three module summative assessments receive a pass for demonstrating competency of the development indicators, scores from the three module rubrics will be weighted and converted to a letter grade.

Competency Components Tasks

Development indicator:

Upon completion of this module, the student who passes will be able to apply basic listening, speaking, reading and writing communication strategies in formal and everyday situations to foster interpersonal relationships.


Knowledge:

Recognize formulas to open and close conversations for personal and professional exchanges.

Recall words and expressions to express sentiments and courtesy for personal and professional exchanges.

Identify appropriate tone for personal and professional exchanges.

Identify French special character letters and symbols.

Associate sounds to the letters of the alphabet.

Identify numbers to 999 in print and in speech.

Recognize abbreviations for personal and professional exchanges.

Recognize connections between elements of the message.


Skills:

Open and close conversational exchanges in formal and informal situations through speaking and writing.

Use and respond to courtesy formulas in conversations.

Apply appropriate tone in conversation.

Express sentiments through speaking and writing.

Respond to short texts through speaking and writing.

Write sentences using structures to convey formal and everyday messages.

Decode and encode using phonetics and spelling conventions.
  • Open and close conversational exchanges
  • Express courtesy
  • Identify and apply appropriate tone
  • Express sentiments


Language Acquisition Strategies

Recall and apply words and expressions to indicate communication difficulties.

Consult a bilingual dictionary as reading and writing strategies.

Use digital autocorrect tools to identify, understand, and correct errors of conventions.

Use contextual cues as a reading strategy.

Isolate words in sentences as a comprehension strategy.

Learning Activities:

  • Guided practice through videos of conversations
  • Task-based scenarios and activities in listening, speaking, reading, and writing (invitations, announcements, voice messages, emails, etc.)
  • Learning capsules to provide modelling and to support tasks

  4. Summative assessment from the facilitator
