Course Description

This course integrates and extends previously introduced concepts of French 1. The course aims to develop competencies in French through authentic learning tasks in preparation for real-life application. Online activities will promote skill development in listening, speaking, reading, and writing in personal and professional contexts. You will use communication strategies to support interpersonal skills, to convey information, and to interact with various forms of persuasive exchanges in routine situations. You will develop skills for life-long learning that are transferable to employment. The targeted second language proficiency of the course aligns with level 3 requirements of the Canadian Language Benchmarks for French, the NCLC.

Eligibility for Prior Learning Assessment & Recognition (PLAR)

Prior Learning Assessment and Recognition (PLAR) is a process a student can use to gain college credit(s) for learning and skills acquired through previous life and work experiences. Candidates who successfully demonstrate mastery of the course competency(ies) may be granted a “pass” for the course. The type of assessment method (s) used will be determined by subject matter experts. Because this is a pass/fail course, the PLAR will be assessed on this basis.

PLAR Eligibility Yes ☒ No ☐

PLAR Assessment (if eligible):

The PLAR will consist of an assessment that is similar to the final summative assessment for the course. Only one attempt will be permitted. If the PLAR is unsuccessful, the student will need to register for and successfully complete the full course in order to earn a pass.

Competencies

A competency is an observable behaviour that successful performers demonstrate on the job. This behaviour is the result of a combination of related knowledge, skills and attributes. Listed below are both the module development indicators (for context) and the course competency.

Course Competency Statement:

Communicate in routine, predictable situations to deal with known topics of immediate personal interest.

Development Indicators:


Module 1: Interpersonal Relationships and Communication Strategies

Apply basic listening, speaking, reading and writing communication strategies in formal and everyday situations to foster interpersonal relationships.

Module 2: Information and Communication Strategies

Apply basic listening, speaking, reading and writing communication strategies in formal and everyday situations to interpret and respond to information of immediate relevance in various contexts.

Module 3: Persuasive Communication Strategies

Apply basic listening, speaking, reading and writing communication strategies in formal and everyday situations to persuade others to act and consider ideas in the context of familiar situations.

Course Skills, Knowledge and Attributes

Skills: A skill is used to carry out a job function to achieve a desired performance result. After initial development, it will then typically be improved with practice.

Skills:

  • Apply knowledge of vocabulary and structures through verbal and non-verbal actions in a conversation.
  • Read and interpret texts using symbol-sound association, vocabulary, structures and strategies.
  • Write sentences using special characters, known vocabulary, structures and strategies.
  • Use digital tools as a learning strategy.

Knowledge: The theoretical or practical understanding of a subject, and/or the ability to apply information in different situations and settings.

Knowledge:

  • Recall vocabulary and structures for general comprehension of conversation.
  • Recognise vocabulary and structures through reading of written texts.
  • Recall symbols, known vocabulary, structures and different types of texts through writing strategies.

Attributes: An attribute is a behaviour that is learned through experience. While attributes are typically not taught directly within a course, it is important to identify those that are considered integral to the successful application of a competency within a job setting.

Attributes:

  • Memorizing
  • Information ordering
  • Pattern identification
  • Verbal development
  • Speech clarity
  • Speech recognition
  • Continuous learning
