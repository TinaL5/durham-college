French 2: Module 3 - Communicate

Apply strategies

Les strategies

  • Use words and structures to help contextualise the meaning.
  • Use visual cues to help understanding.
  • Isolate keywords to help understanding.
  • Use a bilingual dictionary to help understanding and to integrate new word usage in speaking and writing.
  • Use digital tools to help understanding and explore different contextualisations.

Digital tools

Digital tools can help improve your language skills by providing feedback on your writing and providing the pronunciation of words; however, when producing language, try to write as much as you can on your own and use the digital tools to check on things about which you’re uncertain. Especially consider how much you are translating to French and whether the translation is considered your own words. Translation tools can be useful to look up words and expressions and to translate instructions. Try to reserve translation tools for comprehension and avoid them when writing.

Wordreference.com or Linguee

Translate words and expressions with examples of the words used in sentences. Linguee is available as an Android and Apple app.


Collins Conjugaison and Collins app Reverso

Search by verb and listen to the verb’s pronunciation in Collins Conjugaison. Examples of the verb usage with translation are included. The app offers more functionality.


Google Translate

Translate words, sentences, and paragraphs up to 5 000 characters, and documents. Google Translate autocorrects through integration of suggestions in new sentences and pronounces in a standard Canadian accent.
