The verb aller

The verb aller means to go and is an irregular verb, which means that it doesn’t follow a conjugation pattern like regular er ending verbs.

Here is the present tense of aller.

The present tense of aller

The verb aller and greetings

Aller is used to express how you are doing.



Ask How are you?
Comment allez-vous ? (you plural or you singular in a formal context)
Comment vas-tu ? (you singular in a familiar context)

Ask How’s it going?
Comment ça va? (familiar context)

Answer I’m doing well, thank you.
Je vais bien, merci. (a more formal response)

Answer It’s going well, thank you.
Ça va bien, merci. (a familiar response)

When speaking, it is acceptable to respond with a few words, such as très bien, or an expression like pas mal.
