Review of verbs ending in -er and -ir

The conjugation of the verb 'regarder' follow the normal conjugation pattern of regular ‘er’ verbs.

REGARDER TO WATCH / TO LOOK AT

I watch / I am watching
(informal)

You watch / You are watching

He or she watches / He or she is watching

We watch / We are watching
(formal and singular/plural)

You watch / You are watching

They watch / They are watching

The conjugation of the verb ‘sortir’ does not follow the normal pattern of regular ‘ir’ verbs.

SORTIR TO GO OUT

I go out / I am going out
(informal)

You go out / You are going out

He or she goes out / We go out / He or she is going out / We are going out

We go out / We are going out
(formal and singular/plural)

You go out / You are going out

They go out / They are going out

The conjugation of the verb ‘finir’ follow the normal pattern of regular ‘ir’ verbs.

FINIR TO FINISH

I finish
(informal)

You finish

He or she finishes / We finish

We finish
(formal and singular/plural)

You finish

They finish
