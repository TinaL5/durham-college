Let us have a look.

Here is the present tense of the verb ‘s’appeler’

Singular Plural

Je m'appelle - My name is

Nous nous appelons - Our name is

Tu t'appelles (fam.) - Your name is

Vous vous appelez (prl/pol) - Your name is / your names are

il s'appelle - His name is
elle s'appelle - Her name is

Ils s'appellent - Their names are
Elles s'appellent - Their names are

For example:

Je m’appelle Michelle.

My name is Michelle.

Tu t’appelles Pierre.

Your name is Pierre.

Il s’appelle Jean.

His name is Jean.

Elle s’appelle Helene.

Her name is Hélène.

The Verb 'Etre'

The verb etre means to be and it is an irregular verb, which means that it doesn’t follow a conjugation pattern like other, or regular ‘RE’verbs.

The verb etre is also used when you are introducing yourself or others as well as speaking about one’s professional activity.

Here is the present tense of etre.

I am

You are (familiar)

He is

She is

We are

We are

You are (plural or singular form)

They are (masculine or at least one man and one woman)

They are

For example:

Je suis Michelle, je suis comptable.

My name is Michelle, I'm an accountant.

Il est Jean, il est le responsible de Marketing.

His name is Jean, he is the Marketing supervisor.

Elle est Hélène, elle est enseignante.

Her name is Hélène, she is a teacher.
