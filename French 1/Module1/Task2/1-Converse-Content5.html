The Verb ‘S’appeler’

The verb ‘s’appeler’ means to call or to be called. The verb is used when introducing yourself or others.

This verb is a reflexive verb which is accompanied by its reflexive pronouns (me, te, se, nous, vous, se).

Singular Plural

For example:

Je m'appelle Michelle.

My name is Michelle.

Tu t’appelles Pierre.

Your name is Pierre.

Il s’appelle Jean.

His name is Jean.

Elle s’appelle Helene.

Her name is Hélène.

Asking question with the Verb ‘S’appeler’

A complete sentence contains at least a subject and a verb. To ask a question, you can invert the verb and subject pronoun.
‘Vous vous appelez comment?’ is inverted to comment vous appelez-vous?

  • It is not common for inversions to be used with the subject pronoun je.
  • If the 3rd person singular verb does not end in -d or –t, then a -t is placed between the verb and the subject: Comment s’appelle-t-elle?

Definite and Indefinite Articles

Nouns may be described as a word which names a person, place, animal, object or quality.

In French, articles are used before nouns; they can either be a definite article or an indefinite article.


The definite article usually indicates something or someone specific. For example: The book, The girl.

The indefinite article is usually general in nature. For example: A pen or A boy.

The verb être

The verb etre means to be and it is an irregular verb, which means that it doesn’t follow a conjugation pattern like other, or regular, verbs.

Here is the present tense of etre.

I am

You are (familiar)

He is

She is

We are

We are

You are (plural or singular formal)

They are (masculine or at least one man and one woman)

They are

The verb etre and introductions

The verb etre is also used when you are introducing yourself or others.

For example:

Je suis Michelle.

My name is Michelle.

Tu es Pierre.

Your name is Pierre.

Il est Jean.

His name is Jean.

Elle est Hélène.

Her name is Hélène.
