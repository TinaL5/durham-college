Subject matter experts:

Competency:

Communicate in routine, predictable situations to meet basic needs and carry out daily activities.

Development indicator:

Apply basic listening, speaking, reading and writing communication strategies in formal and everyday situations to support interpersonal relationships.

Description:

This course is meant to be a first introduction to the French language. The course aims to develop competencies in French through authentic learning tasks in preparation for real-life application. Online activities will promote skill development in listening, speaking, reading, and writing in personal and professional contexts. You will use communication strategies to support interpersonal skills, to convey information, and to explain and respond to messages in familiar situations. You will develop skills for life-long learning that are transferable to employment. The targeted second language proficiency of the course aligns with level 2 requirements of the Canadian Language Benchmarks for French , the NCLC.

Estimated Duration:

14 hours

Learning Objectives and Tasks

Listening, speaking, reading, and writing are integral to language learning and are practised together to reflect authentic communication contexts. The objective of this module is to develop the following skills.

  • Use memorized words and expressions in speaking and writing to identify, describe, organize, and respond to information.
  • Apply sentence structures and verb conjugation strategies in speaking and writing to identify, describe, organize, and respond to information.
  • Interpret information to provide details in different types of text-based material and conversational-based messages.
  • Respond to short texts through speaking and writing.
  • Decode and encode using phonetics and spelling conventions.
  • Apply appropriate tone in conversation.

Skills are supported with knowledge and strategies and developed by conversing, connecting, and communicating through the following tasks.

  • Identify information and describe information
  • Organize information
  • Respond to information
