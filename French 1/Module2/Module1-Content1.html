French 1: Module 1

Communicate

Structures in language are parts that are put together to make meaning of speaking and writing. Even though language is guided by conventions, it is still flexible. Think about how different people may communicate in different contexts. Writing an email to your supervisor requires a different form than writing a text to a friend, and another person may use slightly different structures in similar writing contexts. Those structures can even vary depending on the cultural context. Conventions for structures can help us respond appropriately and convey messages that are comprehensible. The intention of language is communication.
