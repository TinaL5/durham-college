Venir de + Infinitive

Venir de + Infinitive means to have just done something.

In order to construct the ‘passe recent’ (the recent past) The present tense of the verb ‘venir’ is used, followed by the preposition ‘de’ and then the infinitive of the verb.

See below how it is constructed.

Subject pronoun VENIR DE + INFINITIVE

je

viens

de

déménager.

I have just moved.

tu

viens

de

déménager.

You have just moved.

il/elle/on

vient

de

déménager.

He/she/we have just moved.

nous

venons

de

déménager.

We have just moved.

vous

venez

de

déménager.

You have just moved.

ils/elles

viennent

de

déménager.

They have just moved.

For example:

Je viens de voir les statistiques.
Tu viens de partager les strategies.
Marc vient de visiter la ville de Québec.
Suzanne et Helene viennent de finir la réunion.
