Constructing sentences through structures | Venir de + Infinitive

Venir de + Infinitive means to have just done something.

In order to construct the 'passe recent' (the recent past) The present tense of the verb 'venir' is used, followed by the preposition 'de' and then the infinitive of the verb.

See below how it is constructed.

Subject pronoun VENIR DE +INFINITIVE

viens

déménager.

I have just moved.

déménager.

You have just moved.

déménager.

He/she/we have just moved.

Nous

venons

de

déménager.

We have just moved.

Vous

venez

de

déménager.

You have just moved.

Ils/elles

viennent

de

déménager.

They have just moved.

For example:

Je viens de finir les devoirs.

Tu viens de regarder un film.

Marc vient de visiter la ville de Québec.

Suzanne et Helene viennent de finir la réunion.

A complete sentence contains at least a subject, the person or thing doing, and a verb, what the person or thing is doing.

Constructing sentences through structures

The Preposition ‘à ’ has multiple meanings. The most common meanings are ‘at’, ‘to’ and ‘in’.

The preposition ‘à ’ is used to express location, for example, a town or a city.

For example: Je vais à Ottawa. - I am going to Ottawa.

This preposition is also used when stating different locations in a town or city.

à + le= au

à + la = à la

à + les= aux

For example:

Je vais à le cinéma.

Je vais au cinéma.

I am going to the cinema.

Tu vas à la gare.

Tu vas à la gare.

You are going to the train station.

Ils vont à l’école.

Ils vont à l’école.

They are going to school.

Nous allons à les réunions.

Nous allons aux réunions.

We are going to the meetings.
