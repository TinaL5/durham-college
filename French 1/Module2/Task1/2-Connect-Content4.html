Constructing sentences through structures

Venir de + Infinitive means to have just done something.

In order to construct the 'passe recent' (the recent past) The present tense of the verb 'venir' is used, followed by the preposition 'de' and then the infinitive of the verb.

See below how it is constructed.

Subject pronoun VENIR DE + INFINITIVE

Je

viens

de

déménager.

I have just moved.

Tu

viens

de

déménager.

You have just moved.

Il/Elle/On

vient

de

déménager.

He/she/we have just moved.

Nous

venons

de

déménager.

We have just moved.

Vous

venez

de

déménager.

You have just moved.

Ils/Elles

viennent

de

déménager.

They have just moved.

For example:

Je viens de finir les devoirs.

I just finished my homework.

Tu viens de regarder un film.

You just watched a movie.

Marc vient de visiter la ville de Québec.

Marc just visited Quebec City.

Suzanne et Helene viennent de finir la réunion.

Suzanne and Helene just finished the meeting.

Aller + Infinitive = Le Futur Proche

The 'Futur Proche' is used to speak about actions which will take place in the near future. It corresponds with 'going to...' in English.

In order to construct the 'futur proche' (the near future) The present tense of the verb 'aller' is used, followed by the infinitive of the verb.

See below how it is constructed.

Subject pronoun ALLER + INFINITIVE

Je

vais

parler.

I am going to speak.

Tu

vas

parler.

You are going to speak.

Il/Elle/On

va

parler.

He is / she is / we are going to speak

Nous

allons

parler.

We are going to speak.

Vous

allez

parler.

You are going to speak.

Ils/Elles

vont

parler.

They are going to speak.

Other examples:

Je vais travailler.

I am going to work.

Tu vas lire un livre.

You are going to read a book.

Marc et Helene vont parler de médias sociaux.

Marc and Helene are going to talk about social media.

Exercice: Choisissez la bonne réponse.
