Constructing sentences through structures | Negation

Ne...pas - Not

In order to construct sentences with a negative structure. We should place ne before the verb and pas after it.

Please note:

Let us have a look.

For example:

Affirmative sentence: Je porte - I am wearing...

Negative sentence: Je ne porte pas - I am not wearing...



Je vais au travail. - I am going to work.

Je ne vais pas au travail. - I am not going to work.



In the example above, we see the verb 'aller' being used. Ne goes before the conjugated part of the verb 'aller' then pas comes right after.

Negation continued | Ne... pas de

When changing an affirmative sentence to a negative sentence, we should exclude the number, the indefinite article (un, une, des) or the partitive article (du, de la, des) which follows the verb and replace it with the preposition de.

Let us have a look.

Affirmative sentence: J’ai deux frères. - I have two brothers.

Negative sentence: Je n’ai pas de frères. - I do not have any brothers.



For example:

Tu portes des mitaines dehors.

Tu ne portes pas de mitaines dehors.



J’ai une tuque.

Je n’ai pas de tuque.

Constructing sentences through structures | The verb mettre

The verb mettre means to put or to put on and it is an irregular verb, which means that it does not follow a conjugation pattern like other regular ‘re’verbs.

Endings for -re verbs



Here is the present tense of mettre.

I / I put

You (familiar) / You put

He / He puts

She / She puts

We / We put

We / We put

You / You put (plural or singular formal)

They / They put (masculine or at least one man and one woman)

They / They put

Par exemple:

Je mets mes bottes. - I am putting on my boots.

Vous mettez vos mitaines.- You are wearing your mittens.

Constructing sentences through structures

A complete sentence contains at least a subject, the person or thing doing, and a verb, what the person or thing is doing.
