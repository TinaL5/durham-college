Constructing sentences through structures | Est-ce que?

Let us examine another way of turning a statement to a question. This is done by using ‘Est-ce que’ at the beginning of a statement.

For example:

Statement
French English

Vous regardez la télévision tous les jours.

You watch the television every day.
Question
French English

Est-ce que vous regardez la télévision tous les jours?

Do you watch the television every day?

Exercise using the structure “est-ce que...”

By using the subject pronoun indicated at the end of each statement. Create questions using the given structure ‘Est-ce que’.

For example:

Example
French English

Aimer les cours de francais. (Tu)

Est-ce que tu aimes les cours de francais?

The verb prendre

The verb prendre means to take and is an irregular verb, which means that it doesn’t follow a conjugation pattern like other regular ‘re’ verbs.

Here is the present tense of prendre.

The present tense of prendre
French English

I take

You (familiar) take

He takes

She takes

We take

We take

You (plural or singular formal) take

They (masculine or at least one man and one woman) take

They take

Prendre is used to state what meal one is having or intend to order.

For example:

Examples
French English

Je prends un sandwich.

I am having a sandwich.

Elle prend du jus d’orange.

I am having orange juice.

A complete sentence contains at least a subject, the person or thing doing, and a verb, what the person or thing is doing.
