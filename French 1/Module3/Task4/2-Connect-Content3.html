Negation et des expressions avec avoir?

The Preposition 'à' has multiple meanings. The most common meanings are 'at', 'to' and 'in'.

When expressing aches or pains in French the following expression is used: 'Avoir mal à + the body part'.

Note carefully: The preposition 'a' is used right before the body part. Once 'à' goes before the definite article (le, la, les) it may require the words to contract.

For example:
à + le = au
à + la = à la
à + les = aux

For example:
J'ai mal à la tete - My head hurts.
Tu as mal au dos - Your back hurts.
Elle a mal aux pieds. - Her feet hurt.

Negation continued - Ne... pas de

When changing an affirmative sentence to a negative sentence, we should exclude the number, the indefinite article (un, une, des) or the partitive article (du, de la, des) which follows the verb and replace it with the preposition de.

Let us have a look.
Affirmative sentence: J'ai une fièvre. - I have a fever.
Negative sentence: Je n'ai pas de fièvre. - I do not have a fever.
