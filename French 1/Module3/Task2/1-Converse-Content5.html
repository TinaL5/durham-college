Constructing sentences through structures | Adjectives

POSITION OF ADJECTIVES

Most French adjectives follow the noun they modify.

Exception: There are a few adjectives which refer to beauty, age, and size that usually precede the noun.

Constructing sentences through structures | Adverbs

Generally, adverbs provide information about verbs. Adverbs of time tell when or in what order something ought to happen.

Let us have a look at some of the most common adverbs. Notice, they do not end in –ment.

For example:

Toujours

Always

Parfois

Sometimes

Tard

Late

Tôt

Early

Tout de suite

Immediately

Please Note: Adverbs of time usually follow the verb, but they are also often used at the beginning of a sentence.

Il arrive toujours en retard.

He always arrives late.

Parfois, Pauline et Marc vont au cinema ensemble.

Sometimes, Pauline and Marc go to the movies together.
