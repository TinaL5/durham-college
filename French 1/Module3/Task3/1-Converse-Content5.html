The verb faire

The verb faire means to make and do and it is an irregular verb, which means that it doesn’t follow a conjugation pattern like other, or regular, re verbs.

Here is the present tense of faire.

The verb faire

Faire is used to express the state weather conditions

Quel temps fait-il? | What’s the weather like?

The 3rd person singular of the verb faire is used when stating weather conditions.

Words and expressions
French English

Il fait beau

It is beautiful weather

Il fait chaud

It is hot

Il fait froid

It is cold

Il neige

It is snowing

Il y a de verglas

There is black ice
