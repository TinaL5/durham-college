The Imperative

This is used when making a request or giving a command. The imperative is usually formed by using the tu, vous or nous forms of the present tense without the use of the subject pronoun.

Please note that when constructing the imperative, the ‘s’ in the ‘tu’ form of the ‘er ‘verb is dropped.

For example:
Finis ton repas! - Finish your meal!
Parle plus fort, s’il te plait! - Speak louder please!
Attendez une minute, s’il vous plait! - Wait a minute please!

Faire

The verb faire means to make and do and it is an irregular verb, which means that it doesn’t follow a conjugation pattern like other, or regular re verbs.

Here is the present tense of faire.
