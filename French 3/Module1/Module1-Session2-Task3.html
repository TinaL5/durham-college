Task 3 | Asking Questions using the Futur Tenses

Welcome!

When making reservations or plans we often use the future tenses and we also have to ask questions to obtain or confirm information. This task will help you review how to ask questions using the futur simple and the futur proche.

Learning Goals

  • Identify when to use the futur simple and the futur proche
  • Ask questions in the future tenses

REMEMBER | Flash Grammar

There are three ways to talk about future events in French.

In the previous examples:

  • Le restaurant ouvre à 11 heures.
    Using the présent simple shows that this is a planned event, that is part of an ”official” schedule.
  • Le restaurant ouvrira à 11h30.
    The futur simple can be used to make a simple prediction. For instance, it is 11am and the restaurant has not opened its doors yet. You call to ask when the restaurant will open; an employee answers: Le restaurant ouvrira à 11h30.
  • Le restaurant va ouvrir à 11h35
    "Va ouvrir" is the futur proche. It is used to talk about events that are about to happen. For instance, it is now 11h30 and you guessed it, the restaurant is still closed. The employee sees you coming, comes to the door and tells you: Le restaurant va ouvrir à 11h35.

GRAMMAR | Forming and Using Questions in the Future

How to form questions in the futur simple?

You can form the questions by using the following structures:

With a question word Without a question word

Question word + verb + hyphen + subject

Verb + hyphen + subject

Quand mangerons-nous au restaurant?

Quand mangera-t-elle au restaurant?

Prépareras-tu une salade?

Préparera-t-il/elle/on une salade?

Remember to add « t » between the verb and the subject if the subject is il(s), elle(s), on



How to form questions in the futur proche?

You can form questions in the futur by using the following structures:

With a question word Without a question word

Question word + aller (in the present simple) + hyphen + subject + verb

Aller (in the present simple) + hyphen + subject + verb

Quand allons-nous manger au restaurant?

Quand va-t-elle manger au restaurant?

Vas-tu préparer une salade?

Va-t-il/elle/on une salade?

Remember to add « t » between the verb and the subject if the subject is il(s), elle(s), on
