Topic 1 - Module 1 Overview

Welcome to module 1, which focuses on communicating in daily activities. If you desire to learn French to interact with colleagues in casual conversations or to brush up on your skills in preparation for a trip, this module is for you. It will help you learn key vocabulary, complete realistic communication tasks, and practice your French skills in varied interactive conversations. This module provides over 200 phrases to communicate about daily living in the present and future tenses.

There are five thematic sessions, that are divided into four tasks. In each session, the fourth task is mostly in French in order to provide an immersive opportunity where you can work and communicate only in French. English translations of the learning instructions are available; however, we encourage you to rely primarily on the French instructions as this will help build your ability to perform tasks in a French speaking virtual environment.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

  • Interpret simple texts or audio communications in relevant social, educational, and work-related situations.
  • Communicate using varied basic sentence and grammar structures, connected discourse, the present and future tenses.
  • Write short communications related to daily living.

Assessment Overview

This module includes a formative and a summative assessment. The formative assessment will allow you to prepare for the summative one as they both target the same types of tasks.

Understanding Your Final Assessment

The final assessment consists in the written and video assignments described below.

  • Prepare a 2-minute video where you give instructions on how to plan for a trip or cook a simple recipe (300 words).
  • Write a short email including the instructions to plan a trip or cook a simple recipe (150 words).

Learning Goals

In this assessment your goal is to:

  • Give step by step instructions orally
  • Write step by step instructions
  • Use the present tense, the future and the imperative
