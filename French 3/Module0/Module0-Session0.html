About This Course

Topic 1 - Welcome to French 3

Welcome to "French 3"

Welcome to French 3. This course is an excellent preparation for those who would like to increase their French skills in order to better communicate at work, in academic projects or even on a trip to a French speaking country. It provides over 600 phrases to communicate in short, yet effective sentences that are needed to perform tasks in daily living, professional and academic contexts.

Topic 2 - Course Overview and Schedule

Course Overview and Schedule

The course is designed to guide you step-by-step into practicing and improving your French skills. It uses a task-based approach that allows to practice the French language in real-life scenarios. It includes 13 themed sessions broken down into four 45-minutes tasks and a final three-hour review session.

The sessions are grouped into the three following modules:

  • Daily living
  • Communicating at work
  • Collaborating at work

Each module includes a formative and a summative assessment to help you test your skills. You will need to successfully pass the summative assessments to complete the course.

Objectives

1

Interpret simple texts or audio communications in relevant social, educational, and work-related situations.

2

Communicate using varied basic sentence structures, limited grammar structure, connected discourse and the past, present and future tenses.

3

Write simple to moderately complex communications about concrete topics related to professional and daily living.

Best Way to Study This Course

A great approach to studying this course is to complete one task a day as this helps improve memorization of the key vocabulary, pronunciation, and grammar structures.

Completing a task should take up to 45 minutes; and there is no need to review previous vocabulary as warm-up and review activities are included within the study time required for a task. In addition to that, each task targets only 10 words to help retain vocabulary efficiently and avoid information overload.

If you can’t commit to a task per day, you may elect to complete two at a time. In any case, we highly recommend practicing at least twice a week. This will give your brain some time to process the new information learned which will in turn make your learning more durable.

This course includes resources to help you enhance your French vocabulary and pronunciation. 

Pronunciation Guide

This guide will help you identify how to pronounce each French sounds. It includes audio and equivalent English sounds that you can use as a reference, when applicable.

Glossary

Use the glossary for easy reference from English to French for the 600 words targeted by this course. 

Translation

  • Linguee: This website boasts 1 billion translations. The advantage of Linguee is that it provides contextual translation that allows to see how the word is translated in several real sentences.
  • Deep-L: This tool allows to translate words, texts and even documents. It is one of the most accurate to date and can help you save valuable time when translating documents for an academic or professional project, for instance.

Pronunciation

  • Google Search

    This Google functionality can be used to hear how to pronounce French words. Below are the steps you can follow to do so.

    1. Type in any French word in Google search and press enter.
    2. You will be redirected to the Results page, that usually displays the word, its definition, and an audio icon.
    3. When you click on the audio icon, you will hear how to pronounce the word in French.

      4. Note: Please ensure that the language parameters are set to French in order to obtain search results in French.
