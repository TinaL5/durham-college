Course Description

The progressive acquisition, reinforcement, and creative use of language structures will increase the student confidence to self-expression. With a strong focus on using the language to communicate in real-life scenarios, this course enables students to participate in simple conversations, make requests, suggestions or short presentations and express their opinion about present and past events. Tasks from both personal and professional settings will be used as formative and summative assessments.

Competencies

A competency is an observable behaviour that successful performers demonstrate on the job. This behaviour is the result of a combination of related knowledge, skills and attributes.

Course Competency Statement:

Communicate on familiar concrete topics as well as past, present or future events by interpreting and sharing information, providing instructions or giving presentations. The competency will be demonstrated in personal and professional settings using formal and non-formal registers of language.

Course Skills, Knowledge and Attributes

Skills: A skill is used to carry out a job function to achieve a desired performance result. After initial development, it will then typically be improved with practice.

Knowledge: The theoretical or practical understanding of a subject, and/or the ability to apply information in different situations and settings.

Knowledge:

  • Vocabulary to talk about daily life activities
  • Vocabulary to discuss professional activities
  • The present, future and past tenses
  • The imperative
  • Basic and polite question structures
  • Time, manner and frequency adverbs
  • Expressions of size and quantity
  • Logical connectors

Attributes: An attribute is a behaviour that is learned through experience. While attributes are typically not taught directly within a micro-credential, it is important to identify those which are considered integral to the successful application of a competency within a job setting.

Attributes:

  • Use listening strategies: before, during and after listening
  • Use writing strategies before, during and after the writing process
  • Use oral communication strategies before, during and after speaking
  • Use reading strategies: before during and after reading

Evaluation Process

In order to assess progress throughout the course, the student will need to complete three formative assessments. These will not factor into the final course pass/fail outcome, but will instead help the student monitor their learning progress through the course. Any formative assessment that does not earn a “successfully completed” status will need to be repeated until that status has been achieved. A student cannot progress to the next portion of a course until the formative assessment(s) in the previous section have been successfully completed.

As a competency-based course, the course’s overall pass/fail assessment will be based solely upon three summative authentic assessments (typically an integrated group of tasks), which the student will be required to complete to demonstrate their mastery of the course competency. If a pass is not earned the first time the student attempts the assessment, a second attempt will be permitted. If the second attempt is not successful, the student will be required to re-take the course in its entirety and at the student’s cost.

Important Note: The final summative assessment must be successfully completed by the course end date. No extensions to this date will be provided unless accompanied in advance by an approved accommodation form. Therefore, students are strongly encouraged to attempt this final assessment at least two weeks prior to the course end date, in order to ensure sufficient time is available if a second attempt is required.

Summative Course Assessment

In order to pass the course, three summative assessments must be successfully completed prior to the course end date in order to demonstrate mastery of the competencies. If it is not completed successfully, a second attempt will be permitted. If this is also unsuccessful, the course will be graded as a ‘fail’.

These summative assessments evaluate the three following competencies:

Module 1 | Competency

Communicate through oral or written communication by interpreting and sharing information about present and future events, as well as providing instructions to complete daily living activities.

Competency descriptors

  • Identify the main ideas and supporting details of a simple communication that includes step by step instructions.
  • Communicate using short sentences, basic grammar structure, the present and future tenses and limited but adequate vocabulary.
  • Writes short, simple texts about familiar topics or situations related to daily life.

Module 2 | Competency

Communicate through oral or written communication by interpreting and sharing information about professional and academic topics as well as past events.

Competency descriptors

  • Interpret simple texts or audio communications in predictable, practical and relevant social, educational and work-related situations.
  • Communicate using varied basic sentence structures, limited complex grammar structure, connected discourse, the past, present and future tenses and limited but adequate personal and professional vocabulary.
  • Write short, simple to moderately complex descriptions, and communications about concrete topics related to professional and daily life.

Module 3 | Competency

Communicate through oral or written communication by interpreting and sharing information about professional and academic topics as well as past, present and future events. This stage of the competency will be demonstrated in personal and professional settings using formal and non-formal registers of language.

Competency descriptors

  • Interpret moderately complex texts or audio communications in predictable, practical and relevant social, educational and work-related situations.
  • Communicate using varied basic and a few complex sentence structures, limited complex grammar structure, connected discourse, the past, present, future and conditional tenses, adequate personal and professional vocabulary as well as proper language register.
  • Write short moderately complex descriptions, and communications about familiar, concrete topics related to professional and daily life experience.

Learning Plan

Module 1: DAILY LIVING

Estimated Duration: 15 hours

Communicate through oral or written communication by interpreting and sharing information about present and future events, as well as providing instructions to complete daily living activities.

Competency Components Related Module Topics

Skill:

  • Interpreting, speaking, reading, and writing sentences to:
    • Give instructions and directions for everyday activities and processes
    • Read and follow the steps of a simple recipe
    • Read and follow short, simple directions to a location
    • Fill a form
    • Write to a friend about a familiar situation
    • Write a note to a landlord

Knowledge:

  • Apply the "present progressif"
  • Apply the "imperatif"
  • Apply pronouns “y and en”
  • Apply “les verbes pronominaux”
  • Apply “les pronoms directs”
  • Apply “les pronoms indirects”
  • Making a reservation at a restaurant
  • Giving instructions for going grocery shopping
  • Reading and following recipes
  • Writing a short recipe
  • Talking about daily activities
  • Asking for and indicating one’s way
  • Reading an itinerary of vineyard tours (Quebec and France)
  • Completing a form to rent a vacation property
  • Writing to a friend to describe the vacation house/apartment being considered for a group trip
  • Going to medical appointment
  • Completing family-related activities or personal errands

Learning Activities:

  • Watching videos
  • Listening to audio conversations
  • Reading articles
  • Completing grammar and vocabulary learning activities (Fill in the blanks, Drag and drops, Matching, Sequencing, MCQ, Image MCQs, Audio MCQs, etc.)
  • Listening to and repeating phrases or sentences

Evaluation:

Formative assessment:
Read the request from a friend who enjoys one of your specialty dish. Write the steps of the recipe. Prepare a 2-minute video where you give instructions on how to prepare you’re the dish.  

Summative assessment:
Write the steps to planning a trip. Prepare a 2-minute video where you give instructions on how to plan for a dream trip.
