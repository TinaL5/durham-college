GRAMMAR | Using relative pronouns

When talking about information we have received, we can add a clause to give more precision about them. This clause usually starts with a relative pronoun. Let’s take a look at them.

What are they?

Relative pronouns are used to introduce relative clauses. They replace a noun or a pronoun to avoid repetition.

Below is a list of the main relative pronouns and when to use each.

Four main elements/parts to a course learning outcome:
Relative pronoun Use Condition Example

Qui

To give more information about a person

The person is the subject in the initial sentence.
Jeanc vit à Montréal.

Tu me parles de Jean qui vit à Montréal.

Que

To give more information about a person

The person is the direct object of a verb.
Tu connais Jean.

Tu me parles de Jean que tu connais.

Que

To give more information about an object

The object is an object of a verb.
J’ai donné un livre à Jean.

Le livre que j’ai donné à Jean est intéressant.

Dont

To give more information about a person

The person is indirect object of a verb.
Je te parle de Jean.

Jean, dont je t’ai parlé, vit à Montréal.

Dont

To give more information about an object

The object is an indirect object of a verb.
Je te parle du livre de Jean.

Le livre dont je t’ai parlé est à Jean.

Où

To give more information about a location

Jean travaille à Montréal.

Montréal est la ville où Jean travaille.
