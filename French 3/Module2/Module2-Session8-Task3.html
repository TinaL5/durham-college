GRAMMAR | Using Different Tenses

When coordinating a project, you may have to describe the actions that have taken place, are occurring, are established, or planned.

Let’s have a look at the tenses you can use to do so.

Past actions

Usually, the perfect tense is preferred to provide an account of the actions that have or have not been completed.

« Nous avons livré le plan de formation et nous avons créé la maquette des supports formation. »



Present actions

There are two types of actions that can be described.


The present continuous is used to talk about actions occurring at the time of speech (or when a communication is being written).

« Nous sommes en train de terminer les supports de formation. »


The present simple is used to describe actions that are established as per the project plan.

« La durée de cette tâche est de 10 jours. La livraison des supports de formation a lieu le 5 janvier. »



Future actions

The future simple is used to describe actions that are planned.

« Nous animerons la formation dans 30 jours, comme prévu. »

COMMUNICATE | 10 Minutes

WRITE | Providing a Project Status

Complete the following task.

Draft an email to provide a status to Mme Laurent about the design and delivery of her Mandarin language training program.

Write at least 5 sentences in French using the information below:

  • Destinataire: Mme Laurent
  • Statut :
    Création de la présentation PowerPoint – complété
    Rédaction du guide du participant – en cours
    Livraison des supports de formation – le 5 janvier
    Démarrage des cours de conversation – le 15 janvier

WRITE | Providing Your Project Status
