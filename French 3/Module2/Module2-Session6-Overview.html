Introduction

Welcome back to your French 3 Course. This course is designed to guide you step-by-step into practicing and improving your French skills.

You are now about to complete module 2, which focuses on communicating in a professional setting. If you desire to learn French to work in a bilingual or French speaking company, this course is for you. It will help you learn key vocabulary, complete realistic corporate communication tasks, and practice your French skills in varied interactive conversations.

Course Objectives

Introduction

This module provides over 200 phrases to communicate about professional and academic topics as well as past events.

Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

  • Interpret simple texts or audio communications in relevant social, educational and work-related situations.
  • Communicate using varied basic sentence structures, limited complex grammar structure, connected discourse, the past, present and future tenses and personal and professional vocabulary.
  • Write short, simple to moderately complex descriptions, and communications about concrete topics related to professional and daily life.

Tasks

This module uses a task-based approach that allows you to practice the French language in real-life scenarios. Below are some of the key tasks you will have to complete as part of your learning journey.

  • Welcoming visitors and asking about their trip
  • Describing your role and responsibilities
  • Making and receiving a phone call
  • Scheduling, rescheduling or cancelling an appointment
  • Attending a job interview
  • Presenting one’s company
  • Small talk: Most appropriate small talk topics in Canada
  • Writing emails 
  • Making, accepting, or refusing a request
